About Ayurveda

What is Ayurveda?

The word Ayurveda is derived from Sanskrit and means “science of life”. Ayurveda is one of the world’s oldest medical systems, originated in India over 5000 years ago. It relies on a natural and holistic approach to physical, mental, and emotional health. “Prayojanam chasya swasthasya swasthya rakshanam aturasya vikara prashamanam cha” (The aim of Ayurveda is to protect the health of the healthy & to alleviate disorders in the diseased.)

The Tridosa

Ether (space), air, fire, water and earth are the five basic elements manifest in human body as three basic principles know as tridosa. (Vata, Pita and Kapha) These tridosa determine individual constitution. These are the forces that govern all the psycho physiological function in the body and mind respectively. They control the body and serve to maintain it in a dynamic state of health. When these vital forces are aggravated the body, mind connection begin to suffer and the disease process begins.

Definition of Health

“Samdosa samagni samadhatu malakriya prasann indriya atma mana swastha ityabhidheeyate”

A healthy individual is one who has balanced doshas, balanced agni (fire), balanced dhatus (tissues), and whose senses, mind, and soul are content and at peace.
